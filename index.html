Massage Techniques

Myofascial release is a technique that opens and stretches the fascia. Fascia is connective tissue found between your skin and muscle, between muscle and muscle, thruought muscles and between muscle and bone.

Hot stone massage has been a healing technique for millennia in cultures all around the world.

Trigger point therapy is a manual massage therapy technique that relaxes muscles by deactivating key "trigger points" in muscles.

Insurance

Medical therapeutic massage covered by insurance

Blue Cross Blue Shield and Regence

If you have Blue Cross Blue Shield or Regence health insurance and have massage benefits I can work with you. I can help with many different common conditions including but not limited to: neck pain, carpal tunnel, shoulder pain, thoracic syndrome, frozen shoulder, back pain, sciatica, knee pain, tension related headaches, plantar fasciitis. Medical massage is therapeutic massage with the intent to relieve pain, facilitate injury recovery and improve joint mobility and function. Techniques used include triggerpoint or neuromuscular therapy, Swedish massage, myo-fascial realease, cupping, stretching, deep tissue massage, and passive active release techniques. Home care for ongoing treatment is given after the massage as well as referrals to other care providers.

Out of network

If you don't have blue cross blue shield or Regence but do have out of network reimbursement as part of your insurance plan it is likely I can work with you as well. You would pay for your appointment out of pocket and I will supply you with a "super bill" which you can then submit to your insurance for re-imbursement.
